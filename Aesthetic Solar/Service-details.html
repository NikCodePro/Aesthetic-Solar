Engineering (PE) Stamps

Professional Engineer (PE) stamps are a mark of approval from a licensed engineer.

Engineering PE Stamps:

  • Strictural Stamps
  • Electrical Stmaps
  • Load Calculation

Purpose of PE Stamps:

Safety Assurance:

PE stamps signify that an engineering design has been reviewed and approved by a licensed professional, ensuring that it meets safety standards and regulations to protect public health and safety.

Compliance Verification:

PE stamps verify that engineering designs and documents comply with relevant codes, standards, and regulations established by government agencies and industry organizations.

Quality Assurance:

PE stamps provide assurance of the quality and reliability of engineering work, giving stakeholders confidence in the integrity and performance of the project.

Legal Requirement:

In many jurisdictions, certain types of engineering projects, such as building structures or public infrastructure, require approval from a licensed Professional Engineer and the affixing of a PE stamp to the associated documents.

Process of Obtaining PE Stamps:

Education and Experience:

To become a licensed Professional Engineer, individuals must typically complete a bachelor's degree in engineering from an accredited program and gain several years of relevant work experience under the supervision of a licensed PE.

Licensure Examination:

After meeting the education and experience requirements, aspiring engineers must pass the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam, followed by the Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam in their chosen engineering discipline.

State Licensure:

Engineers must apply for licensure in the state or jurisdiction where they intend to practice. This process involves submitting proof of education, experience, and examination results, along with any additional requirements specified by the licensing board.

Continuing Education:

Once licensed, Professional Engineers are required to participate in continuing education to stay updated on advancements in their field, maintain licensure, and ensure competency in their practice.
